## 3. Does UIC have a tuition lock for undergrads?

- Status: 200
- Abstained: false

### Answer

Yes! UIC has a **4-year tuition lock** for undergraduates — your tuition rate is guaranteed for your entire entering cohort. This means the tuition you pay as a freshman stays the same through graduation.

For 2025-2026, that locked-in rate would be **$5,712 per semester** for in-state students or **$13,763 per semester** for out-of-state students, plus **$2,445 in fees** each semester.

## 8. Do study abroad classes count as UIC credit or transfer credit?

### Answer

Study abroad classes through UIC programs typically count as **UIC credit**, not transfer credit. This means they're treated as if you took them directly at UIC — they count toward your degree requirements and are factored into your UIC GPA.

## 19. Can freshmen live in ARC?

- Status: 200
- Abstained: false

### Answer

Yes! **ARC (Academic and Residential Complex) is exclusively for first-year students** — it's actually one of only two residence halls at UIC that freshmen can live in, along with James Stukel Towers.

ARC is UIC's most modern residence hall with the best in-building amenities, including a gym inside the building. You'll have traditional or semi-suite room options, with costs ranging from **$6,342** (traditional shared) to **$9,005** (semi-suite private). A meal plan is required for all ARC residents.

Since you're interested in ARC, it's worth noting that it has the strongest academics-housing integration on campus, making it ideal for freshmen who want both community and convenience for their studies.

## 20. Which dorms do not require a meal plan?

### Answer

The dorms that do **not** require a meal plan are:

- **Marie Robinson Hall (MRH)**
- **Thomas Beckham Hall (TBH)**
- **Single Student Residence (SSR)**
- **Polk Street Residence (PSR)**

Since you prefer independent living, these optional meal plan dorms might be a good fit - you'd have more flexibility with your food choices and budget.

## 21. What meal plan is best for commuters at UIC?

- Status: 200
- Abstained: false

### Answer

Since you're a commuter, you have more flexibility than on-campus students who are required to buy meal plans. The **Blaze 160** is specifically designed for commuters at **$2,350/semester** — it gives you 160 swipes plus $750 Flames Fare (works at all campus dining spots) plus 30 exchanges.

However, you might want to consider just paying per meal or getting a smaller Flames Fare balance, especially since you can grab food at the **Market at Halsted** (open 24 hours) or hit up spots like **Chick-fil-A**, **Panda Express**, or **Dunkin** in the Student Center East when you're on campus. The Commuter Student Resource Center in **SCE Suite 245** can help you figure out what makes most sense based on how often you're actually eating on campus.
