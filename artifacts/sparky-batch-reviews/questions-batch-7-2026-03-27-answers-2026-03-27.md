## 15. Which dorms are open to sophomores and above?

- Status: 200
- Abstained: false

### Answer

As a sophomore, you have several housing options beyond the freshman-only dorms:

**Commons North (CMN)** - For sophomores and above, plus transfers. You'd get a private bedroom in a suite with 3-6 residents sharing bathrooms. Costs **$6,510** (3-4 person suite) or **$6,403** (5-6 person suite). It's on east campus with convenient access to dining and the Student Center. Meal plan required.

**Commons West (CMW)** and **Commons South (CMS)** are also available to upperclassmen, along with **Courtyard** and **Single Student Residence (SSR)** which offers apartment-style living for more independent students.

The freshman-only dorms you can't access anymore are **ARC** and **James Stukel Towers (JST)**, which are restricted to first-year students only.

## 16. Which dorm is best for health professions students on west campus?

### Answer

**Academic and Residential Complex (ARC)** is your best bet for west campus housing as a health professions student. It's specifically for first-year students and has the strongest academics-housing integration of any dorm, plus it's the most modern building with a gym inside. The Academic and Residential Complex really lives up to its name for students serious about academics — the built-in study spaces and academic programming make it ideal for pre-health students who want that focused environment.
